<p><ul><li>Ionic</li><li>Nonionic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wool-protective agents market is divided into two main types: ionic and nonionic agents. Ionic agents, which carry a charge, effectively bond with wool fibers, providing protection against environmental damage and enhancing dye uptake. Nonionic agents, on the other hand, lack a charge, allowing for more versatile application without affecting fiber properties significantly. Both types are essential in maintaining wool's quality, durability, and aesthetic appeal, catering to different needs within the textile industry.</p></p>

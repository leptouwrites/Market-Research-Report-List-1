Inorganic flocculants, such as alum and iron salts, work by neutralizing charges and aggregating particles for efficient removal. In contrast, organic flocculants, often derived from natural or synthetic polymers, enhance floc formation and retention through more complex chemical interactions. Both types are crucial for optimizing processes like mineral separation, wastewater treatment, and tailings management, ultimately enhancing operational efficiency and environmental sustainability in mining operations.</p></p>
